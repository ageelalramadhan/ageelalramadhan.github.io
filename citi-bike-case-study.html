NYC Citi Bike Dashboard (2022)

Python · Pandas · Plotly · Streamlit · Kepler.gl · Time-Series
Instacart Citi Bike Rockbuster Influenza Happiness Text Mining
Tools & Libraries
Python · Pandas · NumPy · Plotly · Streamlit · Kepler.gl

Dataset: ~1.7 million trip records from NYC Citi Bike (2022). Columns include start/end station, coordinates, duration, and rider type.

Project Overview

This project analyzes Citi Bike usage patterns in New York City during 2022 to support operational decision-making around bike supply, rebalancing, and seasonal demand planning.

Business Problem

Citi Bike demand fluctuates by location, time of day, and season. The objective was identifying high-demand stations, understanding seasonal usage patterns, and determining how operations should adapt to reduce empty docks and unavailable bikes.

Process

  • Cleaned and validated trip data (dates, station names, ride durations)
  • Aggregated trips by station, day, and month
  • Merged daily trip counts with temperature data
  • Ranked top 10 highest-demand stations
  • Built interactive Streamlit dashboard with geospatial and time-series views

Key Insights

  • Demand is heavily concentrated in central Manhattan stations
  • Trips increase significantly during warmer months and decline sharply in winter
  • Peak demand aligns with predictable commuting windows
  • Winter demand averages roughly half of summer demand levels

1) Seasonality Drives Demand

2) Temperature Strongly Influences Ridership

3) Highest-Demand Stations Concentrated in Manhattan

Recommendations

  • Increase rebalancing frequency at high-demand Manhattan stations
  • Scale staffing and operations seasonally (summer vs winter)
  • Implement targeted early-morning and mid-day rebalancing cycles
  • Use spatial hotspot data to evaluate expansion corridors

Conclusion

Time-series trends, spatial clustering, and demand concentration can guide operational efficiency in mobility systems. Aligning supply decisions with observed demand patterns improves reliability and optimizes resource allocation.
