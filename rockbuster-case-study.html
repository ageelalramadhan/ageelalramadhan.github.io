Rockbuster Strategy Analysis (SQL)

SQL • Tableau • Revenue analysis • Market prioritization

Project Overview

This project uses SQL to analyze Rockbuster’s rental database to identify revenue drivers, customer distribution, and geographic opportunities. The goal was to generate insights that could support a data-informed business strategy for growth and retention.

Problem

Rockbuster needed a clearer picture of which films and customer segments generate the most revenue, where demand is strongest, and how to prioritize markets. The business challenge was to use data to make strategic decisions instead of relying on assumptions.

Data

The dataset includes customer data, rentals, payments, film information, inventory, and geographic location fields. All analysis was completed using SQL queries to join tables, aggregate metrics, and uncover patterns in revenue and customer behavior.

Process

  • Explored schema and identified primary/foreign key relationships across tables
  • Built SQL joins across customer, payment, rental, film, and location tables
  • Calculated revenue by film, category, and customer segment
  • Analyzed customer distribution and revenue concentration by country/city
  • Translated findings into clear business recommendations

Key Insights

  • Revenue is concentrated in a small subset of customers and locations
  • A limited number of film categories and titles generate a large share of total revenue
  • High-value customers are clustered in specific markets, enabling targeted retention strategy
  • Geographic analysis highlights markets with strong demand and expansion potential

Geography Concentrates Revenue Potential

Revenue clusters in a small number of countries, suggesting that market prioritization should focus on the highest-performing regions instead of spreading resources evenly across all markets.

Recommendations

  • Prioritize retention campaigns for high-value customer segments
  • Focus marketing and inventory investment on top-performing categories/titles
  • Target expansion or partnerships in markets with concentrated demand
  • Improve reporting cadence using standardized SQL queries for recurring insights

A Small Customer Segment Drives Disproportionate Revenue

The customer revenue distribution is highly uneven. Rockbuster can improve results by identifying high-value segments and tailoring retention, loyalty incentives, and customer reactivation strategies.

Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates how SQL can directly support strategic decision-making by identifying what drives revenue and where growth opportunities exist. The findings provide a foundation for Rockbuster’s market prioritization, customer retention, and content strategy.
