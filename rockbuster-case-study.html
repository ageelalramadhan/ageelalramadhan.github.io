SQL · PostgreSQL · Tableau · Revenue Analysis

Dataset: 15-table relational database — films, customers, rentals, payments. ~16,000 films, ~600 stores.

Project Overview

SQL analysis of Rockbuster's rental database to identify revenue drivers, customer distribution, and geographic opportunities.

Example SQL — Top Revenue Customers

SELECT cu.customer_id, cu.first_name, cu.last_name,
  co.country, ci.city, SUM(p.amount) AS total_amount_paid
FROM customer AS cu
JOIN address AS a ON cu.address_id = a.address_id
JOIN city AS ci ON a.city_id = ci.city_id
JOIN country AS co ON ci.country_id = co.country_id
JOIN payment AS p ON cu.customer_id = p.customer_id
GROUP BY cu.customer_id, cu.first_name, cu.last_name, co.country, ci.city
ORDER BY total_amount_paid DESC LIMIT 5;

Key Insights

  • India, China, and the US lead in customer volume
  • City-level targeting offers limited value — country focus is more effective
  • Top 5 customers by revenue are highly concentrated — retention ROI is high
