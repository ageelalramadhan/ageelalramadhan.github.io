Project Overview

Instacart needed deeper insight into customer purchasing behavior to improve targeting and marketing. This analysis used order-level transaction data merged with customer demographics to answer four core business questions:

#Business QuestionAnalysis Approach
1When are customers most active? Which times/days drive orders?Order timing analysis + busiest period flags
2What distinguishes loyal customers from new ones?Loyalty flag (order count thresholds)
3How does demographics relate to spending behavior?Age, income, and regional segmentation
4Which customer profiles are highest value?Multi-variable profiling (age × income × frequency)

Key Insight — Missing Values

260,290 missing values in days_since_prior_order sounds alarming but is expected — these are customers placing their first-ever order. Recognizing this saves you from incorrectly dropping rows. Context determines whether missing = problem.

Ordering Behavior — When Do Customers Order?

Three derived variables captured order timing patterns: price_label (Low/Mid/High), busiest_day (Busy/Average/Slow), and busiest_period_of_day (Most/Average/Fewest orders).

Key Insight — Peak Hours

Orders peak at 10:00 AM. Promotions timed to 8–10 AM could capture customers at the moment of highest purchase intent. Late-night discount strategies are unlikely to move volume.

Customer Loyalty Segmentation

Customers were segmented into three loyalty tiers based on mean order number per user. A spending flag was also derived from average item price per user.

SegmentConditionBusiness Meaning
Loyal Customermean order_number > 20Long-term, high-engagement users
Regular Customermean order_number 11–20Established but not yet habitual
New Customermean order_number ≤ 10Recent or infrequent users

Key Insight — Loyalty vs Spending

Loyal customers are not automatically high spenders — some long-term users habitually buy lower-priced products. Combining loyalty and spending flags creates more actionable segments than either variable alone.

Customer Demographics

After merging 206K customer records, four segmentation variables were created to make demographics actionable for marketing strategy.

Key Insight — Regional Segmentation

State-level analysis is too granular — 50 groups with too little data each. Mapping to 4 U.S. Census regions (Northeast, Midwest, South, West) reduces noise while preserving meaningful geographic variation for strategy.

Customer Profiling

Low-activity customers (fewer than 5 orders) were excluded first — removing 1.44 million rows. Each remaining customer was assigned a combined profile:

df_filtered['customer_profile'] = (
    df_filtered['age_group'].astype(str) + " | " +
    df_filtered['income_group'].astype(str) + " | " +
    df_filtered['family_status'].astype(str) + " | " +
    df_filtered['order_frequency'].astype(str)
)
# Example: "Adult | Middle income | Small family | High frequency"

Key Insight — Low-Activity Filter

Excluding customers with fewer than 5 orders removed 1.44M rows (4.4% of data) but significantly improved signal quality. One-time or near-inactive users behave fundamentally differently and would skew segmentation results if included.

Key Findings

Finding 1 — Orders peak between 9 AM and 4 PM.

Instacart is a daytime platform. Promotions and logistics staffing should concentrate on the 9 AM–4 PM window. Peak staffing needed around 10:00–11:00 AM.

Finding 2 — Product price is stable across all hours.

Time-based pricing incentives are unlikely to change spending behavior. Promotions should target segments, not hours.

Finding 3 — Regular customers generate the most order volume.

A loyalty conversion strategy — moving Regular to Loyal — offers the highest revenue upside, as Loyal customers have higher per-order value.

Finding 4 — Adults (30–49) are the dominant segment.

Marketing campaigns should optimize for family-stage purchasing behavior: household staples, fresh produce, and meal planning.

Finding 5 — Middle-income customers are the core demographic.

Core catalog pricing should remain accessible to Middle income households, while premium placement targets High income segments specifically.

Finding 6 — High-frequency customers are the largest single group.

Retention strategies (subscriptions, loyalty rewards) have a larger addressable base than acquisition campaigns — making them higher ROI.
