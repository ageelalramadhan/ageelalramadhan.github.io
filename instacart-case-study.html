Instacart Market Basket Analysis

Project Overview

This project analyzes Instacart customer purchasing behavior to uncover patterns in ordering frequency, product popularity, and customer segmentation. The goal was to translate transaction-level data into actionable business insights.

Problem

Instacart wanted to better understand customer purchasing patterns to improve targeted marketing strategies, optimize product placement, and increase repeat purchase rates. The key objective was identifying which customer segments and product categories drive the most consistent engagement and retention.

Data

The dataset included transactional order data, product information, department categories, and customer behavior metrics. Analysis was performed using Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib) to clean, structure, and explore large-scale purchase data.

Process

  • Cleaned and validated transactional datasets to ensure data consistency
  • Conducted exploratory analysis to understand order distribution and frequency
  • Analyzed reorder rates across product categories and departments
  • Segmented customers based on purchasing frequency and loyalty patterns
  • Visualized time-based trends to identify peak ordering behavior

Key Insights

  • Peak ordering hours were concentrated between late morning and early evening
  • A small number of product categories accounted for a disproportionately high share of repeat purchases
  • High-loyalty customer segments demonstrated significantly higher reorder probability
  • Reorder likelihood increased after customers completed multiple purchases

Conclusion

The analysis demonstrated how customer segmentation and reorder behavior can inform more precise marketing strategies and personalized recommendations. By focusing on high-frequency categories and loyal customer segments, Instacart could potentially improve retention and customer lifetime value.
